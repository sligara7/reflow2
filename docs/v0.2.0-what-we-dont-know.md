# v0.2.0 — what we don't know yet

> For whoever is about to use this for real. Read the four questions; the rest is context.

Everything reflow2 knows about its own weak points came from someone using it and writing down
what fought them. Two blind trials and one self-host probe produced fourteen fixes, all of which
landed in v0.2.0. **None of those fixes has been validated by anyone but their author.**

So this release is frozen on purpose. The point of the next stretch is not more features — it is
finding out whether the fixes hold up when someone who did not write them tries to get work done.

Below are the four things we genuinely cannot predict. If you notice any of them, that is the
data. Everything else is a bonus.

---

## 1. Does the gap list stay quiet?

**What changed.** Three detectors were flooding it. Modelling reflow2's own design — 119 nodes —
produced 25 gaps, of which one was true. After the fixes the same design produces **one gap, and
it is correct**. Two more detectors were demoted from "gap" to "signal" because they fired on
work that was done properly.

**What we don't know.** All of that was measured on one design, by the person who changed the
detectors. Yours has different shape.

**What to watch for.** Does `detect_gaps` stay short enough to read every time, or does it grow
into something you start skimming? *A gap list you skim is worse than no gap list* — that is the
failure the whole layer exists to prevent, so noticing yourself skimming is a real finding.

Specifically worth reporting: a gap that fires on something you did **correctly**.

---

## 2. Is your project's vocabulary the coding-shaped one?

**The concern.** reflow2 claims to design anything, but coding is the domain agents are trained
on, so it is the easy case — and the only one that has been tried. Ask for a rocket and "what are
the artifacts?" has no obvious answer; ask for a 3D-printed object and one artifact should
probably be an `.stl`, and nothing in reflow2 knows that.

**What we don't know.** Whether the `Artifact` / `Verification` / `Release` vocabulary fits what
you are actually building, or whether you find yourself bending it.

**What to watch for.** Are you forcing something into a node type that does not quite mean it?
Is there a kind of thing in your design with nowhere to go? That is the sharpest test of the
"design anything" claim we have, and it is tracked as **BL-16** with no answer yet.

---

## 3. Do you hit the single-writer limit?

**How it works.** The graph is a RocksDB store on disk, and RocksDB allows one writer. A second
`reflow2-mcp` on the same graph exits immediately — loudly and cleanly, no corruption, no
split-brain. Two agents on one design therefore works only if they take turns.

**What we don't know.** Whether taking turns is a mild annoyance or a wall.

**Why it matters more than it looks.** This is the single trigger condition for reopening a
decision that was just made deliberately: reflow2 runs as a local file, not a service. The
service would solve concurrency and cost a permanent piece of infrastructure, and would put your
design graph on a machine you do not control. That trade was judged not worth it **because
nobody has actually been blocked yet**. If you get blocked, say so — it is the evidence that
would change the answer.

---

## 4. Do persisted questions change how you work?

**What changed.** Questions asked about a gap now survive the session, in the wording you saw.
A returning agent reads `open_questions` before anything else, so it should follow up rather
than re-deriving and re-asking. Answering records the reply; if the answer does not change the
design, the question stays visible until the gap is settled.

**What we don't know.** Whether this actually helps, or whether you start ignoring the list the
way people ignore notifications.

**What to watch for.** When you come back after a break, does the agent pick up where you left
off? Or does it still feel like starting over — and if so, what did it fail to remember?

---

## How to report any of this

Your kit has a **report-friction** skill. Tell your agent that reflow2 got in the way and it will
write a report, redacted of your design content — it reports which tool, the argument *shapes*,
node *types* and counts, never your requirements or component names.

The repository is private, so filing an issue will probably fail. That is expected: the skill
falls back to writing `reflow2-friction-<date>.md` in your project. Send those on.

**Rough notes are fine.** The most useful finding so far was somebody writing *"I had to
brute-force fourteen guesses"* — not a diagnosis, just what happened. Say what you were doing,
what you expected, and what it did instead.

---

## What is deliberately not finished

Recorded so you can tell a known gap from a surprise:

| | |
|---|---|
| **The multi-pass extraction pipeline** is not reachable agent-natively (BL-7) — provenance, fuzzy dedup and time-aware resolution do not run |
| **One design per graph.** Multi-project support exists in the core but is not exposed (BL-8) |
| **No published binaries.** You need a checkout to build from (BL-15) |
| **Documents are not in the graph.** reflow2 cannot yet tell you a doc disagrees with the design (BL-26) |
| **Nothing has been built end to end with it.** No project has gone concept → running with reflow2 holding the design throughout. That, not a feature list, is what a 1.0 would need |
